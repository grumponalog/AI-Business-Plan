01 — Executive Summary

The opportunity, in one breath

Small businesses know AI matters but have no idea how to use it past ChatGPT. Vinyl has spent years building real AI systems — brains, teammates, automations — for its own agency and clients. We're packaging that into a product: we help small businesses install AI that does real work, guided by people who've already done it.

Delivery is our strength. The gap is commercial — sharpening what we sell and how we say it, then putting it in front of enough of the right people. The market is large and underserved: ~31,800 employer businesses sit within the 60-mile catchment, and almost none have adopted AI meaningfully.

What we sell

One product, four service types — Training, Coaching, Building, Consulting — customized per client via reusable AI teammates and systems.

Who it's for

Owner-led for-profit businesses, ~5–50 people, within 60 miles of Ashland.

Why us

Built for real on real engagements — agency-grade quality, not AI slop, with local trust.

05 — Ideal Customer Profile

Who we're built to win

One primary ICP drives the offer and the messaging; a secondary profile is the path into larger orgs. Everything else is out of scope on purpose.

Primary ICP — "The Owner-Operator"

Firmographics

  • For-profit, 5–50 employees (sweet spot 10–40)
  • Revenue ~$1M–$15M, established 5+ years
  • Within 60 miles of Ashland
  • Manufacturing, healthcare, professional services, trades, distribution, ag-adjacent — anything back-office-heavy

The buyer

  • Owner / founder / president, 40s–60s
  • Decision-maker with budget; can say yes fast
  • Not technical; wears every hat
  • Senses AI matters, doesn't know how to use it

Buying triggers: can't find or afford the next hire; drowning in repetitive admin/marketing/sales work; "no one but me can do this"; tried ChatGPT and was underwhelmed; worried competitors are pulling ahead. What they value: time back, done-with-you delivery, plain language, local trust, and proof it works.

Secondary ICP — "The Department Champion"

An operations or marketing leader / VP inside a 50–250-person org who's excited about AI but lacks solo buying power. They can't close on the day — so the play is the AI Blueprint: 1:1 time that gets the decision-makers into the room. Higher ceiling, longer cycle.

Anti-ICP (disqualify fast)

  • AI-native operators who already build systems
  • Large orgs with an AI council / dev team (theory-stuck)
  • Pre-revenue startups with no budget
  • Shop-floor / Industry-4.0 automation seekers
  • Price-shoppers with no urgency

Fit scorecard (quick qualify)

  • For-profit, 5–50 employees?
  • Owner in the room with budget?
  • Felt pain around hiring / capacity?
  • Back-office work to automate?
  • Within ~60 miles (or remote-willing)?

4–5 yes = pursue now. 2–3 = nurture. 0–1 = pass.

Where to find themThe existing 10-year client/contact list (split for-profit), chambers of commerce (Richland already engaged), BNI & industry associations, Ashland University & economic-dev groups, LinkedIn, and referrals from early wins.

07 — Positioning & Hook

One sharp message

"AI for your business" is too broad. The leading candidate, pending Wednesday's bullseye exercise, is "install an AI employee instead of hiring" PROPOSED — concrete, maps to a salary budget, and sidesteps the vocabulary problem. Supporting rings: "work on, not in, the business," "you're using a rocket ship to go to the grocery store," "your competitors are figuring this out right now," and "AI without the slop."

The pre-launch (give, don't take)

Before any ad runs, split the 10-year list (IF takes nonprofit, Vinyl takes for-profit) and send a short feedback-asking email — "we've gone deep on AI; what are you trying to figure out?" 10 — How We Deliver

A system that runs the engagement

The Blueprint is a working Claude plugin: a prospect books → the strategist gets a Slack mention → a Notion pipeline + intake is created → /discovery prep builds the brief and brand demo → /blueprint strategy drafts the blueprint → the strategist reviews → Claude Design produces the deck. Delivery scales because teammates and systems are built once and customized.

  • Two-meeting Blueprint: Meeting 1 is discovery; Meeting 2 is the reveal + live demos + plan + close.
  • Human-in-the-loop: AI does the heavy lifting; humans review and finalize. The standard: you can't tell it's AI.
  • Eat our own dog food: make Vinyl AI-first internally first (Colleen builds per-teammate "builders," ~2×1hr/week for a month).
Input neededDelivery capacity — how many active engagements can the team support at quality? This caps the growth curve; it sets the delivery cost and new clients/mo in the model.
